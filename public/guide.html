Part One

What CANA is, and what it isn't

CANA is a guided conversation tool for couples. Each of you answers a set of questions across eleven areas of life — faith, marriage, intimacy, children, work, money, and more — and writes a short letter imagining your life ten years from now. CANA then gathers it into a shared plan you can talk through together.

Your answers stay on your computer

This matters, so it's worth saying plainly:

✦

Everything you type lives only on your own Mac. Nothing is uploaded, and there is no account in the cloud.

✦

CANA works fully offline. It never needs the internet to do the assessment.

✦

The password you set is a soft lock to keep partners' entries separate on a shared computer — it is not a bank-grade vault. Anyone with full access to your Mac could reach the data, so don't reuse an important password here.

💡

Good to know: CANA is a tool for reflection and conversation. It is not therapy, counseling, or a clinical assessment. If something difficult surfaces, it may be worth talking with a pastor or counselor.

Part Two

Downloading the app

CANA is downloaded from its releases page on GitHub — think of it as the official shelf where each version of the app is kept.

1

Open the releases page

Click this link to go to the downloads page: CANA Releases. It opens in your web browser (Safari or Chrome).

📷

Screenshot needed

The GitHub releases page, with the latest "CANA" release shown at the top and the green "Latest" tag visible.

2

Find the download file

Under the latest release, look for a section called Assets. Inside it you'll see a file whose name ends in .dmg — for example CANA-4.23.0-arm64.dmg. That .dmg file is the app installer.

📷

Screenshot needed

The expanded "Assets" list with the .dmg file highlighted (the one ending in -arm64.dmg).

3

Click it to download

Click the .dmg file name. Your browser will download it — this takes a moment, as the file is around 90 MB. When it's done, you'll usually find it in your Downloads folder.

📷

Screenshot needed

The browser's download bar/area showing the .dmg finishing downloading.

🖥️

About "arm64": CANA is built for Apple-silicon Macs (M1, M2, M3, M4 — any Mac from late 2020 onward). If you have a much older Intel Mac, let the developer know.

Part Three

Installing it on your Mac

Installing means copying the app into your Applications folder. On a Mac this is a simple drag-and-drop.

1

Open the downloaded file

Double-click the .dmg file in your Downloads. A small window opens showing the CANA app icon next to a shortcut to your Applications folder.

📷

Screenshot needed

The opened .dmg window showing the CANA icon and the Applications folder shortcut side by side.

2

Drag CANA into Applications

Click and hold the CANA icon, drag it on top of the Applications folder, and let go. That copies the app onto your Mac.

📷

Screenshot needed

Mid-drag: the CANA icon being dragged toward the Applications folder, with the arrow between them.

3

Eject the installer

You can now close the little window. In a Finder window, find CANA in the sidebar (under Locations) and click the small ⏏ eject symbol next to it. You can also drag the original .dmg from Downloads to the Bin — you don't need it anymore.

Opening it the first time

1

Open your Applications folder

In Finder, click Applications in the left sidebar, then find CANA in the list.

Double-click the icon. The app opens to a welcome screen. The very first time, macOS may briefly ask "CANA is an app downloaded from the internet. Are you sure you want to open it?" — click Open. This only happens once.

Part Five

Using CANA together

CANA is designed for both of you. Each partner answers the questions and writes their own letter — independently, so your honest answers aren't shaped by seeing the other's.

1

Choose Full Assessment

From the home screen, start a Full Assessment. (A Quick Check-In is a shorter version for revisiting things later.)

📷

Screenshot needed

The CANA home screen showing the Full Assessment and Quick Check-In options.

2

Answer the questions, one chapter at a time

Questions come a few at a time, grouped into eleven chapters. For each, slide to where you honestly land. Not sure what a question means? Tap the small ⓘ beside it for a plain-language explanation.

If a question genuinely doesn't apply to your season of life, switch on Not applicable — it'll be left out of your results.

📷

Screenshot needed

A question screen showing the answer scale, the ⓘ info button, and the "Not applicable" toggle.

3

Write your letter from the future

After the questions, you'll each write a short letter as if it's ten years from now and life has gone well. There are gentle sentence-starters if you're not sure how to begin. Write naturally — a few honest sentences is plenty.

📷

Screenshot needed

The "letter from the future" writing screen with the sentence-starter prompts.

4

Hand the Mac to your partner

When the first partner finishes their questions and letter, the second partner does the same. Once both are done, CANA can generate your shared plan.

📷

Part Six

The optional AI writing (you can skip this)

CANA works completely on its own. But if you'd like the vision, mission, and goals written in warmer, more personal language, CANA can use a small AI model that runs entirely on your Mac — your words never leave the computer. This part is optional and a little more technical.

✓

You can ignore all of this. Without the AI, CANA still gives you every score, every conversation prompt, and a complete written plan. The AI only changes the wording, not the substance.

1

Install Ollama (the local AI engine)

Go to ollama.com, download it for Mac, and install it like any app. Ollama is a free, well-known tool for running AI models privately on your own computer.

📷

Screenshot needed

The ollama.com download page for macOS.

2

Let CANA download the model for you

In CANA, open Settings → Local AI. If Ollama is running, you can tap Download model and CANA fetches the recommended model with a progress bar — no Terminal needed. (It's a one-time download of a few gigabytes, so use good Wi-Fi.)

📷

Screenshot needed

CANA's Settings → Local AI screen with the "Download model" button and progress bar.

3

Refresh a report whenever the AI improves

On any current report there's a ↻ Refresh AI output button. Tap it to re-write the vision, mission, and goals with the AI — your scores and answers stay exactly the same. Handy if you set up the AI after first generating a report.

📷

Screenshot needed

A report screen with the "↻ Refresh AI output" button visible at the top.

Part Seven

Your report, and saving a PDF

Once both of you are done, CANA shows your shared plan: a joint vision and mission, each person's individual vision, a health score for all eleven areas, goals for one, five, and ten years, the tensions most worth talking about, and gentle insights.

1

Read it together, slowly

This is the heart of CANA — not the numbers, but the conversation they open. The "Top Tensions" are simply the places where your answers differ most; they're starting points for talking, not verdicts.

📷

Screenshot needed

The CANA report screen showing the joint vision and the domain-health chart.

2

Save a PDF to keep

Tap Save as PDF on the report. macOS opens a print window — choose Save as PDF at the bottom and pick where to keep it. This PDF is your permanent keepsake, and the safest way to back up a report.

📷

Screenshot needed

The macOS print dialog with the "Save as PDF" option highlighted at the bottom-left.

📄

The PDF is neatly laid out with one section per page — vision, future letters, differences, health, goals for each horizon, tensions, and insights.

Part Eight

Getting future updates

When a newer version of CANA is released, the app can tell you.

1

Check for updates

On the home screen, use Check for updates. If a newer version exists, CANA opens a simple step-by-step guide in your browser that downloads it and walks you through replacing the old version. Your saved data is never touched by updating.

Part Nine

If something goes wrong

A few common situations and what to do.

🔄

The app won't open or looks blank. Quit CANA fully (⌘ Q) and open it again. Your data is saved and won't be lost.

🧠

The AI writing didn't appear. Make sure Ollama is running and a model is installed (Part Six), then use ↻ Refresh AI output on the report. The plan works fine without it either way.

💾

Moving to a new Mac? Your data lives on the computer where you used CANA — it doesn't travel automatically. Save important reports as PDFs (Part Seven) before switching computers.

⚠️

Still stuck? Reach out to whoever shared CANA with you, and include a photo of any message you see on screen — that makes it much easier to help.
